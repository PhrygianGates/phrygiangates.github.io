# Formulate Draft Part

## Workflow

1. Read the target draft first. If the user asks to match existing language, read nearby related posts before editing.
2. Preserve the original logical order and all substantive ideas. Do not replace the author's reasoning with a new structure unless asked.
3. Expand omitted, compressed, or incomplete steps only enough to make the argument readable as prose.
4. Correct technical inaccuracies silently by writing the correct statement. Do not mention that the draft was wrong.
5. Treat the text as part of an article, not necessarily a complete article. Avoid forced introductions, conclusions, and summaries.
6. Remove task-context traces such as references to a course, chat, transcript, or earlier draft unless the author explicitly wants them.
7. Keep terminology close to the author's style. Mixed Chinese/English technical terms are acceptable when already used locally.
8. Prefer direct Markdown prose with display equations. Avoid over-explaining familiar background unless a missing step blocks the argument.
9. Keep edits scoped to the requested section or file. Do not clean unrelated wording or restructure neighboring sections without need.

## Output Standard

The result should read like the author's own blog section after careful formulation: clearer, complete enough, technically correct, and still recognizably based on the original draft.
